Buyer guide

What to check before selecting an outdoor LED billboard

Buyers comparing outdoor LED billboard suppliers in Bangladesh usually focus first on cabinet size or headline price, but that is rarely enough to judge total value. A more practical review looks at visibility, weather readiness, installation method, operating cost, and local support capacity over time.

1. Pixel pitch and viewing distance

A smaller pixel pitch gives sharper visuals at closer range, while larger pitch options are often more practical for roadsides and long-distance viewing. The display should be chosen according to actual site distance, not only budget.

2. Brightness for Bangladesh daylight

Outdoor visibility is critical in direct sunlight. Confirm that the screen is designed for daytime readability rather than relying on generic display specs that suit indoor installations only.

3. Cabinet durability and waterproofing

Rain, dust, heat, and humidity put pressure on outdoor installations. Ask about enclosure quality, structural support, and whether front or rear maintenance access is appropriate for the location.

4. Power and maintenance planning

Long-term cost is influenced by power usage, replacement part availability, and service response time. A slightly cheaper screen can become more expensive if maintenance is difficult or parts are delayed.

5. Supplier capability

Installation quality matters as much as hardware. Evaluate whether the supplier can handle site survey, structure guidance, content setup, and ongoing support. For local commercial projects, that often matters more than headline specifications.

6. Warranty and after-sales coverage

Ask how service tickets are handled, what warranty exclusions exist, and whether local technicians are available. Businesses should treat support readiness as a buying factor, not an afterthought.

Project planning

What a stronger procurement brief usually includes

A better procurement brief normally lists screen location, expected viewing distance, operating hours, mounting method, content type, and support expectations. That makes vendor comparison much easier and reduces the chance of choosing an overbuilt or underperforming setup.

For commercial buyers, it also helps to request a site visit or installation recommendation before final approval. That tends to produce better screen sizing decisions than relying on generic package pricing.

Selection framework

How businesses usually narrow down the right setup

Start with the location, not the screen model. Measure sightline distance, check sunlight exposure, review mounting constraints, and define how often content will change. Then compare suppliers based on local project handling, installation method, and service availability.

FAQ

Questions buyers often ask

How much brightness is usually needed for an outdoor billboard?

It depends on site exposure, but outdoor screens generally need high brightness levels to remain visible in direct daylight and open roadside conditions.

Should every project use the smallest pixel pitch available?

No. Pixel pitch should match viewing distance. Paying for unnecessary resolution may not improve practical performance for roadside or long-distance viewing.

Is local maintenance support important?

Yes. Outdoor screens operate in demanding conditions, so fast maintenance access and parts availability can reduce downtime significantly.

Can the same supplier support both permanent and temporary setups?

Some suppliers can, but the right choice depends on whether your priority is fixed branding, campaign rotation, or event-based display use.
